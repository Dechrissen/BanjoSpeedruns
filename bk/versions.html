Banjo-Kazooie Version Differences

N64 Region Differences

  • NTSC-U 1.0 is the fastest version.
  • PAL is a bit slower.
  • NTSC-U 1.1 is slower.
  • NTSC-J (1.1) is the slowest version.

Xbox Version Differences

  • This document details the differences in the Xbox release of Banjo-Kazooie.

Main Differences

  • Framerate

    PAL runs at 25 FPS, NTSC at 30 FPS. However, unlike other PAL region video games, Rare decided to enhance their PAL versions to (almost) match NTSC's movement speed. This makes PAL a great version to speedrun unlike other PAL N64 game versions like Super Mario 64 or Ocarina of Time

  • Languages

    You can choose language on PAL, it's english only on NTSC-U. So it's faster to reset on NTSC after MMM. On PAL, german have the shortest texts, and french the longest texts (basically around 1s difference in spiral mountain) On NTSC-J, it's japanese only, which make the texts slower than other versions.

  • Level Cutscenes

    The cutscene when you open levels is faster on NTSC.

  • First Backflip

    The first backflip in spiral mountain is very hard on PAL, You have to go behind the corner of the wall to attempt it.

  • Swimming

    Swimming is faster on PAL. But turning underwater is faster on NTSC.

  • Flying

    You turn faster when you fly (with kazooie or with the bee) on NTSC.

  • Enemy Speed

    Enemies are slower on PAL. For example on NTSC: after MMM you can't make a straight line to the gate because ripper goes faster, Or after underwater honeycomb in TTC snacker can be more annoying

  • Juju

    Juju spins faster on NTSC.

  • GV Lobby shockpad

    The shockpad in gobi's valley lobby goes faster around the vase on NTSC.

  • TTC Early

    Clipping through the cannon for TTC early is easier on PAL. The setup on NTSC is very consistent, but you can yolo it on PAL and still clip.

  • Gobi Clip

    Gobi clip (to go inside jinxy) is easier on PAL. Rat-a-tat-rap on the black line on PAL. Be stuck in talon trot on the black line on NTSC. You can also do the beak barge strat on both version (which is very hard). You can do gobi clip the NTSC way on PAL with the boots.

  • Beak Bomb Clips

    On certain 90ish Degree connecting floors and walls, you can beak bomb clip through them without lag on PAL. This allows for many out of bounds clips that aren't possible on the NTSC versions of the game. This is most noticeable on PAL NSO as that platform has essentially no lag and it allows you to do beak bomb clips only exclusive to laggy versions of the game.

  • FP Lobby OoB

    The clip to do FP early/450 skip/350 skip is easier on PAL.

  • CCW softlock backup

    The CCW flower softlock backup is different on PAL, because beakbarges are longer.

  • RBA

    On NTSC you clip through the 765 note door. On PAL, a bit harder, you clip through the top right of the right tree (or top left of the left tree).

  • Bee clips

    Most of the bee clips are harder on PAL. The clip in spring to go in the room at the top of the tree in CCW with bee is very precise on PAL The clip to go in engine room in RBB with the bee is harder on PAL BGS early with bee is less easy on PAL.

  • RBB window clips

    Window clips in RBB with the bee are easier on PAL.

  • DoG skip

    DoG skip is easier on PAL.

Smaller Differences

  • Demo cutscenes

    The demo cutscenes before entering in the file menu are different.

  • File 1 lag

    Entering in file 1 on NTSC is laggy and 0.5s slower than files 2 and 3. There is no difference here on PAL.

  • Lag

    NTSC is most of the time more laggy than PAL on laggy places.

  • First fade-out

    To time runs exactly: the first frame of the cutscene (just after the fadeoute when you enter in a new file) is at 3.94 on NTSC (add 0.5 for file 1) and at 4.02 on PAL.

  • Text

    Text scrolls faster on NTSC.

  • Beakbomb

    You beak bomb slightly higher and for a longer distance on NTSC.

  • Beakbarge

    Beakbarge is slightly longer on PAL.

  • Hourglass Timer

    The timer can be slightly different on both version. For example, it's impossible to enter the church in MMM with 5 on the timer on NTSC, but it is possible on PAL. On the contrary, it's impossible to get a 51 tumblar on PAL.

NTSC Only

  • Flutter cancel on flat ground

    Flutter cancels are faster on NTSC. You can't flutter on PAL pressing A twice quickly on flatground. The way to save time in PAL is to flutter before touching the ground after a jump.

  • GV OoB

    To go out of bounds in gobi's valley with the bee, you can clip through the tip above the door on both versions (the door to get the stop 'n' swop egg, where gobi gives you the honeycomb). But you can't clip through the door on PAL (under the tip).

  • MMM Well OoB

    You can't go out of bounds in the well in Mad Monster Mansion on PAL.

NTSC 1.1 Only

  • Termite Skip Patch

    Termite skip is "patched" on NTSC-U 1.1 and NTSC-J (frame perfect mid air jumps are required, but is still technically possible RTA). You need the termite transformation to climb tickers tower, so 5 more tokens.

  • CCW Flower Softlock Patch

    In CCW Spring, the flower allows the player to plant extra eggs into it to progress the flower twice in Spring. This softlocks the game, but you can escape the softlock by taking damage from bees to navigate to Mumbo's Hut blindly. You then could grab the flower jiggy in Spring instead of Autumn, which saves a bit of time. This was patched on NTSC-U 1.1 and NTSC-J.

PAL Only

  • CC OoB

    Going out of bounds in Clanker's Cavern, clipping behind the slope (which makes you go to the alcoves) in PAL only (afaik).

  • FF OoB

    Going out of bounds in FP is very very harder on NTSC, by beak bombing through the blue present with honeyhive on.

  • Crypt water switch

    In the crypt after MMM, you can push the water level switch and destroy the coffin with 1 same ground pound sometimes on PAL.

  • Navigation room with bee

    Window clip to navigation room with the bee only works on PAL. You can use grimlet clip to go in this room on NTSC.

  • TAS stuff

    50 skip, 180 skip, 260 skip, and 810 skip are PAL and TAS only.
